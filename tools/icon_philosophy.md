# Design Philosophy — "Soft Pixel Warmth"

**Philosophy:** Friendliness rendered with restraint. A single beloved character, distilled to its
most essential, instantly-readable form, resting in a field of calm pastel light.

**Form & space:** One subject, centered, generous breathing room. The cat's silhouette is built from
soft rounded masses — a chubby head, two confident triangular ears — so the shape alone is recognizable
the instant it appears, even shrunk to a sixteen-pixel speck in a taskbar. Nothing competes for
attention; the negative space around the cat is part of the composition, a quiet cushion.

**Color & material:** A gentle vertical wash from lavender to mint — cool, soft, modern — cradles a
near-black cat. Two assertive points of warmth anchor the face: bright spring-green eyes and a single
pink nose. The restraint of the palette (one dark, one cool field, two warm accents) is deliberate and
painstakingly balanced so the icon feels cohesive at every size.

**Scale & rhythm:** The cat fills roughly two-thirds of the rounded frame — large enough to dominate,
small enough to float. The eyes are oversized on purpose: they carry the entire emotional payload, the
detail the eye finds first.

**Craftsmanship:** Every curve is supersampled and resolved with master-level care so edges stay crisp
when scaled down; the rounded frame echoes the modern Windows app-tile language; the highlights in the
eyes are placed with the precision of someone who has drawn this a hundred times. The result should feel
effortless and warm — the product of patient, deliberate refinement, not a quick sketch.
